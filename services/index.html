Service Agreements

We have two types of service agreements: Hourly and Subscription.

Hourly Rate

This is our standard agreement; we bill by the hour based on a set rate. We handle project management and scheduling of releases. The client decides on the features with our help, and then we deliver code. These agreements are strictly time and material projects.

Monthly Subscription

Some companies prefer to go with a "subscription model". Rather than worrying about "hours", our clients worry about "results". We agree to a set of features for a month (called it a sprint) and hit that date. Basically, you pay for the sprint rather than the hours.

What this means is that you now pay for "features" rather than "hours". It helps with business decision making. This lets companies decide if the development is "really worth X amount of dollars"?
